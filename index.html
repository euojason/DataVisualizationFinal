    The blue line represents the global average life evaluation over the selected period.


    Log GDP per capita:
    A measure of a country’s average income, showing that wealthier nations tend to report higher happiness.
    Social support:
    The extent to which people feel they have someone to count on in times of trouble.
    Healthy life expectancy:
    The number of years a person is expected to live in good health, capturing physical well‑being.
    Freedom to make life choices:
    How much freedom people have to make life choices.
    Generosity:
    How common donating or helping strangers is, reflecting a culture of giving.
    Perceptions of corruption:
    How widespread people think corruption is in government and business—lower perceived corruption supports happiness.
    Dystopia + residual:
    A baseline comparison; it represents the happiness predicted for a hypothetical country with the lowest possible scores on the six factors above, plus any unexplained influences.

    Data: World Happiness Report 2011–2025
